### Year 1 vs Year 2 distribution note (A3 — critical ops constraint)

> **Root issue**: cruise-line approved-operator onboarding (RCL, Princess, HAL) takes 6–9 months. "100% existing capability" applies to ops execution — NOT to direct cruise-line distribution channel. Correction below.

| Horizon | Distribution channel | Addressable pax | Commission impact |
|---------|---------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Year 1 (Nov 2026 peak season)** | **Aggregator-only**: ShoreTrips + ShoreExcursionsGroup | ~20–30K pax reachable via aggregator channel (subset of 200K HL cruise pax) | +25–40% commission vs. direct; net margin ~25–35% |
| **Year 2+ (Oct 2027 season onward)** | **Cruise-line direct** (RCL Discoveries + Princess + HAL) — IF approved-operator application filed Sep 2026 and approved (6–9mo lead-time) | ~200K captive pax/season direct access | Lower commission ~15–20%; higher conversion; higher brand trust |

**Revised capability statement**: Ops capability = existing (T10 + T6/T7 100%). Distribution channel = Year 1 aggregator-only → Year 2 direct (subject to cruise-line approval). PO should NOT plan Sep 2026 launch expecting direct cruise-line booking volume — launch on aggregator, file application simultaneously for Year 2 scale.

### Design rationale

**Pain**: Cruise pax (P1 + P7 + P6) spend 6–10h Ha Long then leave — never sleep on bay (§1.2 capture 65–80% HL, but all daytime).
**Evidence**: HL 83 calls × ~2,500 pax avg = ~200K pax/season in VN waters (§1.2); Dec–Jan peak = 36 calls in 2 months (§5.2 port-call density); cruise booking leads retail 2–4 weeks → 8-week conversion window (§5.2).
**Whitespace (§4)**: §4 shore-ex category Whitespace column shows NO comparator offers "overnight stay on HL after disembark" as a cruise-extension SKU. Viator Cruises vertical lists only single-day shore-ex.
**Portfolio**: T10 + T6/T7 = ops capability 100%; Year 1 distribution = aggregator channel; Year 2 = direct pending cruise-line approval filing Sep 2026.
